Use this service if you’re a UK business or organisation in the ETII sector that needs to apply for the energy bills discount scheme support.

Eligible ETIIs 

You must have a non-domestic contract with a licensed energy supplier.

Your UK business or organisation should also be:

  • classed as an ETII operating in an eligible sector – see the list of eligible sectors
  • able to prove that at least 50% of its revenue being generated from UK-based activity is one of the eligible SIC codes

What is not eligible

You will not be eligible for support if:

  • your energy is not supplied via a licenced supplier

The discount will not apply to: 

  • energy used to generate electricity to export back to the grid, including if it has been stored first
  • energy made available to customers in the Republic of Ireland

Before you start 

You will need:  

  • contact details of the applicant (including email address and phone number)
  • name and address of the business or organisation
  • supplier name and meter numbers for eligible meters
  • completed declaration document signed by one of the following:
    • a named director listed on the Companies House apply
    • (if not a company) a named person whose authority in that business or organisation is similar to a director of a company
    • Note: an agent is not permitted to sign the declaration on behalf of a business or organisation
{{ govukInsetText({ text: 'Providing your supplier name and meter numbers during application will reduce the time it takes to receive your discount.' }) }}

You’ll need to provide further information if your business or organisation was not registered at Companies House with an eligible SIC code in December 2022. This includes:

  • a letter signed by a chartered accountant or auditor confirming that you are in an eligible sector, and that 50% or more
  • of your revenue is in an eligible sector
  • your most recent full set of end-of-year accounts - if you do not have these, upload evidence of company accounts over a minimum of 6 months at a minimum
  • an income statement referring to the same time period as the end-of-year accounts
  • a breakdown schedule which separates activities between eligible and ineligible SIC codes
  • a sample of sales invoices & VAT returns which represent the eligible activities

What you need to know

After you apply, your application details and related evidence will be assessed to determine if you should receive the discount.

You’ll be told about this decision by email. You should get a response within X weeks.

If you’re eligible, a certificate will be emailed to you. This will include information on steps you may need to take to get the discount.
