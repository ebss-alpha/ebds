The Windsor Framework and state aid

Beneficiaries based in Northern Ireland

If the beneficiary is incorporated and registered in Northern Ireland, European Union (EU) State aid rules will apply. If you are based in Northern Ireland, select 'No' at the bottom of this page.

Beneficiaries based in Great Britain

In exceptional circumstances, the discount for some Great Britain based beneficiaries could in principle be subject to the Windsor Framework and EU State aid rules would therefore apply. However, this would only be the case where there is a genuine and direct link to Northern Ireland and where the discount will have a foreseeable and material effect on trade between Northern Ireland and the EU.

If this is the case, we will need to consider the Northern Ireland market impacts further with you. This includes:

  • what ringfencing is already in place
  • whether any additional ringfencing measures are needed to ensure that the benefit of this support does not pass through to Northern Ireland

To help identify whether this requirement applies to you, answer the following questions.

  1. If you're a Small and Medium Enterprise (as defined in Section 382 Companies House Act 2006): Based on your energy costs for financial year 2022 to 2023, do you expect your annual energy bill to exceed £14 million?
  2. If you're a large company (as defined in section 465 Companies House Act 2006): Based on your energy costs for financial year 2022 to 2023, do you expect your annual energy bill to exceed £47 million?

If you answered no to questions 1 and 2, the Windsor Framework does not apply. Select 'No' at the bottom of this page.

If you answered yes to questions 1 or 2, answer question 3.

  1. Does your business have more than 10% share of the market in Northern Ireland?

If you answered no to question 3, the Windsor Framework does not apply. Select 'No' at the bottom of this page.

If you answered yes to question 3, answer questions 4 and 5.

  1. Does your business have locations in Northern Ireland, or are you part of a corporate group with locations in Northern Ireland?
  2. If your business is solely Great Britain based but you sell your goods or electricity into Northern Ireland, are those goods sold below market price, or sold at different market conditions?

If you answered no to questions 4 or 5, the Windsor Framework does not apply. Select 'No' at the bottom of the page.

If you answered yes or think the answer might be yes to question 4 or 5, the Windsor Framework may apply. Select 'Yes' at the bottom of the page and we will contact you separately.

Based on your answers to the questions above, might the Windsor Framework apply to you?
